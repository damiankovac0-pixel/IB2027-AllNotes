# A2.1.1 Purpose And Characteristics Of Networks

A computer network connects computers and other devices so they can share data, resources, and services.

Networks are used for:

- communication and collaboration
- sharing files and printers
- accessing online services
- business transactions
- remote work
- online learning
- controlling and monitoring connected devices

## Local Area Network

A local area network, or LAN, connects devices over a small geographical area.

Examples:

- home network
- school network
- office network

Characteristics:

- short distance, often within one building or site
- high data transfer rates
- low latency
- can be wired, wireless, or mixed
- often privately owned and managed

Main purpose: sharing local resources such as files, printers, applications, and internet access.

## Wide Area Network

A wide area network, or WAN, connects networks over a large geographical area.

Examples:

- connecting company branches in different cities
- connecting school campuses
- multinational business networks

Characteristics:

- covers long distances
- often connects multiple LANs
- may use leased lines, fibre, microwave links, satellite links, or internet-based VPNs
- generally higher latency than LANs

## Personal Area Network

A personal area network, or PAN, connects personal devices around one person.

Examples:

- phone connected to wireless headphones
- smartwatch connected to a phone
- laptop connected to a Bluetooth keyboard

Characteristics:

- very short range
- low power
- often uses Bluetooth or Wi-Fi Direct
- designed for personal devices

## Virtual Private Network

A virtual private network, or VPN, creates a secure encrypted connection across a public network such as the internet.

Corporate use:

- allows remote employees to access a private company network securely
- makes the remote device behave as if it is connected to the company network

Consumer use:

- hides or changes apparent location
- encrypts internet traffic between user and VPN provider

Main purpose: secure remote communication over untrusted infrastructure.

## Internet

The internet is a global network of interconnected networks using standardized protocols.

It supports services such as:

- web browsing
- email
- video streaming
- cloud services
- messaging
- online gaming

Important distinction: the internet is infrastructure; the World Wide Web is one service that runs on it using protocols such as HTTP and HTTPS.

## Comparing Network Types

| Type | Scale | Typical technology | Main use |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| LAN | building/site | Ethernet, Wi-Fi | local sharing |
| WAN | city/country/global | fibre, leased lines, VPN | connecting distant LANs |
| PAN | around one person | Bluetooth, Wi-Fi Direct | personal devices |
| VPN | virtual/private over public internet | encryption/tunnelling | secure remote access |
| Internet | global | TCP/IP protocols | global communication |

## Exam Pattern

When describing a network type, mention its scale, purpose, typical technology, and one example. For VPNs, emphasize secure encrypted communication over public infrastructure.
