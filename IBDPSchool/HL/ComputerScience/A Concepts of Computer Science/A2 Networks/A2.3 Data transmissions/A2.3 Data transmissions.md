# A2.3 Data Transmissions

This topic explains how data is addressed, transmitted, split into packets, and routed across networks.

The big idea: data transmission depends on addresses, transmission media, packet switching, and routing decisions. A message does not usually travel as one complete object; it is broken into packets that move through the network and are reassembled at the destination.

## Study Order

1. Learn IP addressing, including IPv4, IPv6, public/private, static/dynamic, and NAT.
2. Compare transmission media: fibre-optic, twisted-pair, and wireless.
3. Understand packet switching: segmentation, headers, routing, reassembly.
4. Compare static and dynamic routing.
