# A3.4.3 OLAP And Data Mining

Online analytical processing, or OLAP, and data mining are used for business intelligence.

Both help organizations extract useful insight from large datasets, but they work differently.

## Online Analytical Processing

OLAP is software technology for analysing business data from different points of view.

OLAP often uses multidimensional data structures called OLAP cubes.

## OLAP Cube

An OLAP cube organizes data across multiple dimensions.

Example dimensions:

- time
- customer
- product
- region
- supplier

This lets users analyse data by different angles.

Example: sales can be analysed by product, by region, by month, or by product and region together.

## Role Of OLAP In Business Intelligence

OLAP supports:

- interactive analysis
- fast summary queries
- dashboards and visualizations
- ad hoc queries
- comparisons over time
- forecasting support
- budgeting and performance analysis

OLAP is useful because data is pre-processed and organized for analysis.

## OLAP Examples

A business may use OLAP to answer:

- Which region had the highest sales this quarter?
- Which product category is declining over time?
- Which customer group spends the most during holidays?
- How do marketing costs compare with sales growth?

## Data Mining

Data mining is the process of finding patterns, relationships, and insights in large datasets.

It may use statistics, machine learning, and artificial intelligence.

Unlike OLAP, data mining is often used to discover patterns that were not already known.

## Role Of Data Mining In Business Intelligence

Data mining helps organizations:

- predict future behaviour
- identify customer segments
- detect fraud
- discover buying patterns
- support marketing decisions
- forecast demand
- assess risk

## Classification

Classification assigns data items to predefined categories.

Example: classifying customers as low-risk, medium-risk, or high-risk.

In business, classification can help target products, detect spam, or assess loan applications.

## Clustering

Clustering groups similar data items without predefined labels.

Example: grouping customers with similar shopping behaviour.

Businesses use clustering to identify target markets or customer segments.

## Regression

Regression predicts a numerical value based on other data.

Examples:

- predicting future sales
- estimating house prices
- forecasting demand
- predicting customer spending

Regression is useful for trend analysis and financial forecasting.

## Sequential Pattern Discovery

Sequential pattern discovery identifies patterns in ordered events.

Example: customers who buy product A often buy product B next week.

This can help predict customer behaviour over time.

## Association Rule Discovery

Association rule discovery finds items or events that commonly occur together.

Example: customers who buy sausages and barbecue sauce may also buy charcoal.

Businesses use this for promotions, product placement, and recommendation systems.

## OLAP vs Data Mining

| Feature | OLAP | Data mining |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Main purpose | analyse known dimensions | discover patterns and relationships |
| Data preparation | usually pre-processed into cubes | may use large raw or prepared datasets |
| Typical question | what happened? | what patterns or predictions can be found? |
| Output | summaries, reports, dashboards | models, predictions, patterns |

## Exam Pattern

For OLAP, emphasize multidimensional analysis and decision support. For data mining, emphasize discovering patterns and making predictions from large datasets.
