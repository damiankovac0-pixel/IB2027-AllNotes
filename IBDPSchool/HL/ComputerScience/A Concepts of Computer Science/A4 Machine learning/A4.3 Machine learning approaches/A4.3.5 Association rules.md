# A4.3.5 Association Rules

Association rule mining finds relationships between items or attributes in large data sets.

It is usually linked to [[A4.1.1 Types of machine learning#Unsupervised Learning|unsupervised learning]] because it discovers patterns without labelled outputs.

## Core Idea

Association rules are often written like this:

```text
A -> B
```

This means that when `A` occurs, `B` is also likely to occur.

Example: customers who buy bread and butter may also buy jam.

## Market Basket Analysis

The classic example is [[A4.1.1 Real-world ML applications#Market Basket Analysis|market basket analysis]], where transaction data is used to find products often bought together.

Uses:

- product recommendations
- store layout decisions
- promotions
- cross-selling
- targeted marketing

## Support

Support measures how often an itemset appears in the whole data set.

High support means the pattern is common.

Example: if `bread and butter` appears in many transactions, it has high support.

## Confidence

Confidence measures how reliable the rule is.

It asks: when the left side occurs, how often does the right side also occur?

Example: for `bread -> butter`, confidence measures how often customers who buy bread also buy butter.

## Lift

Lift measures whether the relationship is stronger than random chance.

- lift greater than 1: items occur together more often than expected
- lift equal to 1: no meaningful association beyond chance
- lift less than 1: items occur together less often than expected

Lift is useful because high confidence alone can be misleading if the right-side item is already very common.

## Minimum Support And Confidence

Association rule algorithms often use minimum thresholds.

- higher minimum support: fewer but more common rules
- lower minimum support: more rules, including rare patterns
- higher minimum confidence: rules must be more reliable
- lower minimum confidence: more rules, but weaker reliability

## Limitations

- correlation does not prove causation
- rules can become too numerous
- rare but important patterns may be missed if support threshold is too high
- privacy concerns can arise when analysing customer behaviour

## Exam Pattern

Association rules uncover relationships between attributes in large data sets. Support measures how common an itemset is, confidence measures how reliable a rule is, and lift measures whether the association is stronger than random chance.
