# A4.4 Ethical Considerations

This topic is about judging the impact of machine learning systems, not just whether they work technically.

Ethics asks whether a system is fair, safe, transparent, accountable, lawful, sustainable, and respectful of people affected by it.

## Syllabus Parts

- [[A4.4.1 Ethical implications of machine learning|A4.4.1 Ethical implications of machine learning]]
- [[A4.4.2 Ethics as technologies integrate into daily life|A4.4.2 Ethics as technologies integrate into daily life]]

## Main Idea

Machine learning systems can make decisions or recommendations that affect real people. This matters most in sensitive areas such as healthcare, education, recruitment, finance, policing, surveillance, and online communication.

The key exam skill is balancing benefits against harms.

## Exam Pattern

For ethics questions, structure answers around:

1. What the system does.
2. Who benefits.
3. Who may be harmed.
4. What data is used.
5. What risks exist.
6. How risks could be reduced.
